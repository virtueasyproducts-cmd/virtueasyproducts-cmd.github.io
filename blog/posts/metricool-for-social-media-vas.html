What Is Metricool?

Metricool is an all-in-one social media scheduling and analytics platform. You connect your clients' accounts, plan content in a visual calendar, schedule posts to go out automatically, and pull analytics to show clients how their content is performing.

It supports Instagram, Facebook, TikTok, LinkedIn, Pinterest, YouTube, X (Twitter), Google Business Profile, and more. As a VA managing multiple clients, that matters because you are not logging in and out of individual platforms — everything lives in one place.

Why Social Media VAs Specifically Benefit From It

Most scheduling tools are built for solo brand owners managing one account. Metricool is built for volume. Here is what that looks like in practice:

  • Multiple brand workspaces: Each client gets their own separate workspace. You switch between them easily without accounts bleeding into each other.

  • Analytics and reporting: Pull performance data for any time period and show clients exactly what is working. This is what moves you from "post scheduler" to strategic partner — and justifies higher rates.
  • Auto-publish: Content goes out without you having to be online. You can batch-schedule a full week or month of content and step away.

How to Use Metricool as a Social Media VA

Step 1: Set Up a Workspace for Each Client

When you create your Metricool account, set up a separate brand workspace for each client you manage. This keeps analytics, content calendars, and settings cleanly separated. Your clients never see each other's data, and you stay organized even as your roster grows.

Step 2: Connect Their Social Accounts

Inside each workspace, connect the platforms your client is active on. Instagram Business, Facebook Page, LinkedIn Company Page, TikTok — whatever is in scope for your service package. Metricool will ask for admin access, so make sure your client has added you as an admin on each platform before you connect.

Step 3: Use the Content Planner to Batch Schedule

The content planner is where you will spend most of your time. Drop in your captions, upload your graphics or videos, add hashtags, and set publish times. The visual calendar view makes it easy to see if you are hitting your posting frequency and if content is spaced out properly across the week.

Batching is the move here. Set aside one or two days a month to schedule everything for a client, rather than logging in daily. This is how you keep your hourly output high and your stress level low.

Step 4: Check the Analytics Before Every Client Check-In

Before your monthly or bi-weekly client check-in, pull an analytics report from Metricool. It will show reach, engagement rate, follower growth, best-performing posts, and more. Bring that data into your update email or call and you immediately look more professional than 90% of other VAs who just send a screenshot of the Insights tab.

Pro tip: Use Metricool's report data to pitch upsells. If a client's Reels are getting 3x the reach of static posts, that is your opening to propose a short-form video package add-on.

What to Include in Your Social Media VA Service Package

Once Metricool is set up, here is a simple starting package you can offer:

  • Content calendar planning (monthly or bi-weekly)
  • Scheduling and auto-publishing across 2-3 platforms
  • Monthly analytics report with key takeaways
  • Caption writing and hashtag research
  • Engagement check-ins (responding to comments, DMs)

A beginner social media VA package runs anywhere from $500 to $1,200 per month depending on the number of platforms, posting frequency, and whether you are writing captions or just scheduling supplied content. As you add analytics reporting and strategy, that number goes up.

Common Mistakes to Avoid as a New Social Media VA

Scheduling without reviewing

Auto-publish is great, but check your scheduled queue before it goes live. A typo in a caption or a broken link is your responsibility, not the client's. Build a quick review habit into your workflow.

Ignoring analytics

A lot of new VAs treat analytics as a bonus, not a core deliverable. It is actually one of the most important parts of the job. Clients who see data keep paying. Clients who only see posts eventually question whether they need you at all.

Managing everything from the client's personal login

Never ask a client to hand over their personal login credentials. Have them add you as an admin or manager on each platform, then connect through Metricool using your own account. This protects both of you.
