Scope creep

It always starts with "hey, quick thing."

You signed on for inbox management. Six weeks later you're also booking her travel, chasing her invoices, and rebuilding the same report every Friday.

Every one of those arrived as "quick thing." Not one was worth making a fuss about. Together they're hours a week you're not paid for.

Roughly how many, if you're honest?

1–2 hrs 3–5 hrs Don't ask

Here's the trap. You can't raise it now, because there's nothing to point at. You'd be arguing from memory against someone who doesn't think she did anything.

A scope of work fixes it, and not by being aggressive. It just gives you something neutral to point at:

"Happy to take that on. It's outside what we scoped, want me to price it?"

Easy with a document behind it. Nearly impossible without one, which is why most VAs just eat the hours.
